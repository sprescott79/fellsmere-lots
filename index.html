A-2 Zoning Explained

What Does A-2 Zoning
Actually Mean?

A lot of buyers aren't familiar with agricultural zoning. Here's the plain-English version of what A-2 zoning in Indian River County allows — and why it matters for the kind of lifestyle you're planning.

🏠

Residential Use

A-2 zoning permits one single-family residence per lot. Build your home your way, on your terms.

🐴

Horses & Livestock

Horses, cattle, goats, chickens, and other livestock are permitted on A-2 zoned properties.

🌱

Agricultural Use

A-2 supports a wide range of agricultural activities — farming, orchards, gardens, and more.

🚫

No HOA

These tracts have no homeowners association. No dues, no approval committees, no restrictions beyond county code.

📏

Your Acreage

At 7–10 acres, you have meaningful space to build, grow, and expand your vision over time.

💡

Utility Flexibility

Most tracts have electric access available. A-2 zoning gives you options — not mandates.

Important: Permitted uses may vary by tract location and are subject to Indian River County regulations. This overview is for general informational purposes only. Buyers should verify all permitted uses, setbacks, and building requirements with Indian River County before purchase. Information deemed reliable but not guaranteed.
